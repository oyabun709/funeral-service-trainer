Section 1

Getting Started

Open the app in any browser — no account, no download, no login required.

1

Open the app

Go to funeral-service-trainer.vercel.app in your phone or computer browser. Bookmark it for easy access — tap the share icon on iPhone and choose "Add to Home Screen" for a dedicated icon.

2

Create your student profile

The first time you open the app, tap Get Started → and enter your name. Your profile stores all your progress locally on your device — nothing is sent to any server except for the AI conversations themselves.

3

Multiple students, one device

The app supports multiple profiles on the same device. If you share a tablet with another student, each person can have their own separate progress. Tap your name in the top-right corner to switch profiles.

4

Your progress is saved automatically

Everything — completed scenarios, quiz scores, scenario attempt history — saves automatically to your device. You don't need to do anything to save your work. Just use the app and your progress will be there next time.

Important: Because your progress is saved on your device (not a cloud account), clearing your browser's data or switching devices will reset your progress. Use the same browser on the same device each time for continuous tracking.

Section 3

Practice Scenarios

The heart of the app. Have real conversations with AI-powered family members and practice your professional skills in a safe environment.

How it works

You play the role of a funeral director. The AI plays a grieving family member with a specific personality, emotional state, and situation. Your job is to respond as you would in real life — with empathy, clarity, and professional compliance. The AI reacts realistically: it responds well to patience and clear guidance, and it becomes withdrawn or frustrated when rushed or met with jargon.

How to run a scenario — step by step

1

Choose a scenario from the library

Tap the Practice tab (◎) at the bottom. Browse the 12 scenarios. Each card shows the category, difficulty level, a description, and your past scores if you've attempted it before. Tap Begin → to select one.

2

Read the prebrief carefully

Before the conversation starts, you'll see a Prebrief screen with the scenario's learning goals, the difficulty level, and any past attempt scores. Read the goals — these are exactly what you'll be evaluated on. Tap Begin Scenario → when ready.

3

Respond as the funeral director

The family member opens the conversation. Type your response in the text box at the bottom and tap the ↑ button (or press Enter). Take your time — there's no time pressure. Write responses as you'd actually speak, not as you'd write an essay.

4

End the scenario when it's complete

When all the learning goals have genuinely been addressed, a green "Scenario Resolved" banner will appear automatically. Tap Debrief → to get your feedback. You can also tap End → in the top bar at any time to stop and get evaluated on what you've done so far.

Tip: The scenario won't resolve until you've actually addressed all the learning goals — not just mentioned them. If the green banner doesn't appear, re-read the goals and think about what's still missing from the conversation.

Difficulty levels

Start with Beginner scenarios to build confidence, then work up.

Beginner

Emotionally straightforward families. Good for building fundamentals and getting comfortable with the flow.

Intermediate

More complexity — skeptical families, compliance questions, cultural sensitivity. Requires more precision.

Advanced

High-stakes, emotionally intense scenarios. Family conflict and child loss. Every word matters.

Section 4

Coach Mode

Get real-time feedback from an AI instructor while you're mid-scenario — not just at the end.

🎓 What Coach Mode does

After each response you send, the AI instructor analyzes your message in the context of the scenario's learning goals and gives you:

  • What you did well or missed in your last response
  • The single most important goal still to address
  • A specific example of what a strong next response could sound like
1

Turn it on from the scenario header

During any roleplay, look for the 🎓 Coach button in the top-right of the scenario screen. Tap it to turn Coach Mode on. It will highlight gold when active.

2

Send a response and watch the coaching panel appear

After you send your message, a yellow panel appears below the chat. Wait a moment — the instructor is analyzing your response. This takes 3–5 seconds.

3

Read the tip, then keep going

The coaching panel shows three things: your tip, your goal focus for next, and a suggested phrase you could try. Use it as a prompt to sharpen your next response. The panel clears when you send your next message so it doesn't distract you.

When to use Coach Mode: Turn it on when you're learning a new scenario type or when you feel uncertain. Turn it off when you want to challenge yourself — it's more realistic to practice without a safety net once you've built confidence.
Note: Coach Mode adds a small delay after each message while the AI analyzes your response. This is normal. The family member's response and the coaching note are separate — the family never sees or is affected by the coaching.

Section 5

Session Debrief

After every scenario, the AI evaluates your full conversation and gives you detailed, actionable feedback.

A–F

Letter Grade

Overall assessment of your performance. A = 90%+, B = 80%+, C = 70%+, D = 60%+, F = below.

⬤⬤⬤

Skill Scores

Scored 1–5 in three areas: Empathy, Clarity, and Compliance (FTC/ethics).

✅❌

Goal Checklist

Each learning goal from the prebrief is checked — did you actually achieve it or not?

💬

Better Phrasing

One specific example of stronger language you could have used, written out for you to study.

What to do with your debrief

1

Read the verdict and summary to understand the overall arc of your conversation.

2

Check which goals were not met (❌) — those are your specific gaps to focus on in the next attempt.

3

Read the "Better Phrasing" suggestion carefully — save phrasing that resonates to use in real situations.

4

Tap Try Again immediately while the feedback is fresh. Repeat until you hit an A.

Score history: Every attempt is saved. When you return to the Prebrief screen for a scenario you've done before, you'll see your last 3 attempts with their scores. Use this to track whether you're genuinely improving.

Section 6

Study Mode

Two tools for learning terminology and preparing for the National Board Examination.

Flashcards

44 key terms across 6 categories. Tap a card to flip it and reveal the definition.

1

Filter by category

Use the category filter bar at the top to focus on one topic area at a time. Categories: All, FTC & Legal, Embalming, Sciences, Grief & Counseling, History & Profession, Death Certificates.

2

Tap the card to flip it

The front shows the term. Tap anywhere on the card to flip it with a 3D animation and reveal the full definition. Tap again to flip back.

3

Navigate with Prev / Next

Use the ← Prev and Next → buttons to move through the deck. The counter in the top-right shows your position (e.g., "5 / 44").

NBE tag: Cards marked "NBE" are specifically relevant to the National Board Examination. If you're actively studying for the NBE, prioritize these.

NBE Practice Quiz

75 multiple-choice questions in NBE format. Filter by topic, answer all questions, then submit for instant grading.

1

Choose a filter (optional)

Run all 75 questions at once, or filter by: Arts, Sciences, FTC & Legal, Embalming, Grief & Counseling, Death Certificates, or History & Profession.

2

Answer every question before submitting

Select one answer per question. The submit button stays locked until you've answered all questions — you can't partially submit.

3

Review your results

After submitting, every question is shown with: your answer, the correct answer highlighted in green, and a brief explanation. Questions you got wrong are bordered in red for easy review.

Quiz history is saved on the Progress tab. You can see every quiz you've taken, what filter you used, and your score — so you can spot topic areas that need more work.

Section 7

Ask the Instructor

Your personal AI professor, available 24/7. Ask any funeral service question and get a clear, concise expert answer.

Meet Professor Chen — an AI expert with 25 years of simulated experience as both a funeral director and NBE prep instructor. Ask him anything in plain English. He'll answer in 3–6 sentences with the clarity of a classroom lecture.

Great questions to ask

FTC & Compliance

"When exactly do I have to give a family the General Price List?"

"What are cash advance items and when do I have to disclose the markup?"

"What does the FTC Funeral Rule say about embalming?"

Science & Embalming

"What's the difference between rigor mortis and livor mortis?"

"What chemicals are used in arterial embalming and what do they do?"

"How does cavity embalming differ from arterial embalming?"

NBE Prep

"What's on the NBE Arts exam vs. the Sciences exam?"

"What are the stages of grief according to Kübler-Ross?"

"Walk me through what happens at an arrangement conference."

Important disclaimer: The instructor provides educational information only — not legal, medical, or therapeutic advice. For real licensing questions, always consult your state board or a licensed attorney.
Ask the instructor your questions before a scenario to make sure you know the content, then go into the roleplay with confidence. It's the best way to combine theory and practice.

All 12 Scenarios

A complete reference. Work through them roughly in order — difficulty increases as you go.

1

Arrangement Conference — First Meeting

BeginnerArrangement Guide an overwhelmed family through their first arrangement meeting. Practice pacing, compassionate listening, and process explanation.

2

FTC Funeral Rule — Price Transparency

IntermediateCompliance Navigate presenting the GPL to a skeptical, financially stressed family member. No steering, plain language required.

3

Direct Cremation — Explaining the Process

BeginnerArrangement Explain direct cremation accurately without steering toward more expensive options. Mention the alternative container per FTC rules.

4

Cultural Request — Navigating Uncertainty

IntermediateCultural Handle a family request for culturally specific arrangements when the family doesn't know every detail. Ask, don't assume.

5

Family Conflict — De-Escalation

AdvancedConflict Manage active disagreement between family members in the arrangement room. De-escalate, don't take sides, redirect to the deceased's wishes.

6

Death Certificate Intake — Staying in Your Lane

IntermediateLegal Collect demographic information while explaining which fields belong to the medical certifier. Never speculate on cause of death.

7

First Call — Removal Authorization

BeginnerFirst Call Handle the initial phone call from a family. Collect information, express condolences, and explain next steps clearly.

8

Aftercare — What Happens Next

BeginnerAftercare Walk a family through post-service next steps. Know your scope — refer out for legal, financial, and estate matters.

9

Pre-Need Consultation — Planning in Advance

BeginnerArrangement Walk a healthy individual through pre-arranging their funeral. Honor their autonomy, explain benefits, document preferences without pressure.

10

Embalming Questions — Informed Consent

IntermediateArrangement Answer a skeptical family member's pointed questions about embalming. Accurate info, no pressure, proper authorization or respectful refusal.

11

Veterans Burial Benefits — Navigating VA Benefits

IntermediateLegal Explain VA burial benefits accurately — what's covered, who does what, and what you cannot promise. Know your limits.

12

Child Loss — First Call — The Hardest Call

AdvancedFirst Call Handle a first call from a parent whose infant has passed. Lead with unconditional compassion. Every word matters. Never rush.

Section 10

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Practice

How to use this tool the way it was designed — for real skill development, not just completion.

Don't just finish — repeat until you earn an A

Completing a scenario once isn't the goal. Read your debrief, note the unmet goals, and try again immediately. The fastest learning happens in the second and third attempt, when you know what's missing.

Write like you talk, not like you write

The AI family members respond better to natural, conversational language than formal or essay-style responses. Type your response as if you were actually speaking to someone in the room. Short sentences, warm tone, real words.

Use "Ask the Instructor" before hard scenarios

Before attempting the FTC Funeral Rule, Veterans Benefits, or Embalming scenarios, ask Professor Chen to explain the topic first. Then go into the roleplay with the knowledge already in your head — that's the sequence that sticks.

Practice Scenario 12 (Child Loss) last

This scenario is emotionally demanding. It's designed for students who have already built confidence with the other scenarios. Approaching it too early — before you've developed the instinct to lead with compassion over process — will result in a poor experience.

Check your Compliance score first

In your debriefs, look at Compliance before Empathy. Compliance errors (FTC violations, speculation on cause of death, practicing law) are the ones that can cause real professional harm. Make sure you're never below a 4 there before moving to harder scenarios.

"The family standing across from you on the hardest day of their life deserves a professional who has already made every mistake — safely, in a training room."

That's what this tool is for. Good luck.
